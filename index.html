Composition & synthesis

Every decision, traced, back to the vision it serves.

Throughline owns the single line of meaning that holds a body of work together. It produces vision-thread artifacts — the canonical, traced articulation of what you are building toward — and wires every downstream decision back to that thread with a declared hook. Direction becomes auditable, not aspirational.

The problem it solves

"We're all aligned" is the most expensive assumption in a portfolio.

Alignment doesn't break loudly. It erodes quietly — one undeclared assumption at a time, one decision that felt consistent but wasn't traced. By the time the drift is visible, it has compounded through a dozen specs, a handful of product bets, and months of work all pointing at slightly different stars. Throughline intervenes structurally: every artifact declares the thread it serves, so the gap between "what we said we were doing" and "what we are actually doing" is machine-readable, not a retrospective conversation.

Vision without traceability is a slogan. A vision-thread with declared hooks is an auditable contract with your future self.
How you use it

Name the thread. Wire the work. Read the drift.

1

Emit the vision-thread

Author the canonical vision-thread artifact — the traced, specific statement of what the portfolio is becoming. Not a slogan. A structured document that coherence scoring can reason against.

2

Hook every decision

Each downstream artifact (spec, decision, plan, signal) declares a thread-hook back to the vision-thread entry it serves. The hook is structural — not a comment, a first-class frontmatter field.

3

Trace and extract

Walk any decision back to the vision it serves in one command. Extract the coherent narrative from artifact clusters for publishing. Contradictions surface as explicit forks — they don't dissolve into consensus.

What makes it different

The conservation law: threads do not merge without supersession.

The rule the whole product is built around

Narrative coherence is not consensus.

Most "alignment" tools either impose a mandate top-down (OKR cascades) or collect back-references voluntarily (decision logs). Throughline refuses both. The thread-hook is a first-class artifact property — every downstream artifact is structurally required to declare which vision-thread entry it serves. Contradictory threads are treated as candidate forks or explicit supersessions, never quietly resolved into averaged language. The output is two traceable surfaces that are never collapsed:

Vision-thread artifacts

The canonical, traced statement of what the portfolio is becoming — authored, versioned, and coherence-scored.

Decision-to-vision trace

The auditable walk from any decision, spec, or signal back to the thread entry it serves. Contradictions surface here, not silently downstream.
